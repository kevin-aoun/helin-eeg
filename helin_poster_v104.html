02 The Backbone
Swap. Don't retrain.
HELIN is built on LaBraM (Large Brain Model), an AI model pretrained on 2,500h of EEG data. To adapt to a new task, we train a small adapter network, just a few megabytes. Once trained, an adapter is reusable: load the one you need, the rest of HELIN stays untouched.
<2MB per adapter Reusable adapters 2,500h pretraining 5.8M params 1 GPU to train
04 Task 02 · Epilepsy
Finding Signal, Faster.
To diagnose epilepsy, neurologists spend hours watching EEG looking for rare spikes that last a tenth of a second. HELIN uses a two-pass approach: the first pass flags every candidate, the second pinpoints the real spikes and filters out artifacts. This cuts down revision time from 30mins to 5mins for 24h EEGs.
In collaboration with Rizk Hospital Neurology · IRB signed
0.907 val AUC 92.9% median per-patient 85.3% test F1 Two-pass pipeline

Challenges Ahead 06
Honest Constraints.
HELIN already generalizes to unseen subjects: that's the point of a pretrained transformer. However, it does come with its own set of challenges.
  • A backbone like Signal-VJEPA could improve cross-subject generalization by predicting in feature space instead of reconstructing noisy signal
  • Clinical rollout at Rizk needs prospective validation
  • Currently runs on a GPU; edge deployment is future work
